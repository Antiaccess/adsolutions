About

While the journalist in me seeks to discern meaningful insights from data, the designer in me utilises divergent and convergent thinking to solve human problems in innovative ways.

Hi. My name's Anthony; thanks for visiting. I've interviewed counsellors and Buddhist monks as a radio journalist, cut lead stories for channel 9 news as a video editor, and have now found a home in UX & product design.

I chase the epiphanies that come from primary research, whether that be through workshops, contextual observation, interviews or usability testing. I organise these insights to identify the real inherent problems, create hypotheses and design solutions that are useful, and intuitive, early in the product design process.

Collaboration is core to my process, like any user-centred designer, and I do my best work when part of a team of passionate people I can exchange knowledge with. I have a keen interest in working on ethical and social impact projects and working on digital applications that are disrupting their surrounding market.

  • Sydney, Australia
  • UX Designer at Link Group (LNK)
  • In the field since 2017
1.

I trust that with time and research, clarity will come. Early on, I run workshops with stakeholders to get alignment, understand context and plan a discovery roadmap. I keep my process transparent, with shared notes documents, while keeping a pulse on teammembers and users.

2.

With some clarity on the problem, and perhaps a hypothesis, I brainstorm out-of-the-box solutions solo and as part of a team. Then cross-pollinating what works with what's feasible and viable, I have concepts to stress-test with internal reviews or prototype testing.

3.

My metric for a high-impact feature is whether or not it adds to a users' wellbeing in a farsighted way - which usually is a win for the business. These become the priority, and form the MVP. Lastly, I build out prototypes, test & measure them, learn and iterate.
